Layer 01 — The External Problem
Customer adoption is not a technology problem.
It is an outcome definition problem.
Without a shared definition of what “done” looks like for the customer, every tool decision is arbitrary and every adoption metric is meaningless.
Problem A
Customer adoption stalls without outcome clarity
What matters most is customer outcome, time to value, and customer value. The tool becomes relevant only at a specific step in the process.
Problem B
Tools are ubiquitous, seasonal, and increasingly expensive
Compute costs for Anthropic, OpenAI, Gemini, and others are rising. Build vs Buy decisions must be sequenced by switching cost and outcome fit.
Problem C
No consistent GTM operating model across the enterprise
Process standardization is not the goal — it is the byproduct of a clear operating model. The goal is consistent customer outcomes at scale.

Every submission is scored using RICE — a standardized framework that converts subjective estimates into comparable, rankable scores. This removes political prioritization and creates a defensible paper trail.
R
Reach
How many people or workflows does this impact in a given period? Larger reach = higher score.
I
Impact
How significantly does this move the needle? Scored 0.25 (minimal) to 3 (massive) against business outcomes.
C
Confidence
How certain are we in our estimates? Expressed as a percentage. Low confidence discounts the score automatically.
E
Effort
How many person-months does this require? Effort sits in the denominator — high effort lowers the score.
RICE Formula
Score = (Reach × Impact × Confidence) / Effort
Higher score = higher priority. Scores are relative — rank ideas against each other, not against a fixed threshold.

Every idea that passes RICE scoring gets a Build vs Buy evaluation before any investment decision. The Cost Intelligence Agent runs all three options and estimates TCO for the full lifecycle — not just year one.
